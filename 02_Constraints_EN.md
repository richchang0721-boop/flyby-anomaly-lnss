## Five Postulates of the LNSS Framework

1. **Locality:** The anomaly arises from a local field structure near Earth, not from distant gravitational sources.
2. **Continuity:** The field varies smoothly with position and time (no discontinuities).
3. **Coupling to spin:** The field is generated by rotating mass; a non-rotating Earth would produce no LNSS.
4. **Open orbit selectivity:** The anomaly appears only in open (hyperbolic) orbits, not in closed orbits.
5. **Weak-field limit:** The field is consistent with all known gravitational tests in the weak-field regime.

The full-dataset ap vs dV correlation (r = −0.72) includes a confound: Juno simultaneously has high ap (29) **and** low |P₂| (0.031). The true cause of Juno's null result is geometry, not ap. Removing null cases reduces ap correlation to −0.36.
